Formulas on Quadratic Equations :

  • The roots of the Quadratic equation x=(-b ± √ D /2a, where D = b2-4ac

  • Nature of roots :
        D > 0, roots are real and distinct(unequal)
        D = 0 ,roots are real and equal(coincident)
        D < 0 , are imaginary and unequal

  • The roots α+iβ),(α-iβ) are the conjugate pair of each other

  • Sum and product of roots : if α and β are the roots of a Quadratic equation,then
        S = α+β = -b/a = -coefficient of x/coefficient of x2
        p = αβ = c/a = constant/coefficient of x2

  • Quadratic equation in the form of roots : x2-(α+β)x + (αβ) = 0

  • The Quadratic equation a1x2+b1x+c1=0 and a2x2 + b2 x + c 2=0 have;
        one common root if (b1c2 - b2 c1/c1a2 - c2 a1) = (c1a2 - c2 a1/a1b2 - a2 b1)
        Both roots common if a1/a2 = b1/b2 = c1/c2

  • In Quadratic Equations ax2+bx+c=0 or [(x+b/2a)2 - D/4a2] =0
        if a>0, minimum value = 4ac - b2/4a at x = -b/2a.

Formulas on Graphs :

  • Slope of a line
       Formula : Slope (m) = (Change in y) / (Change in x)

  • Linear Interpolation
       Linear interpolation between (x1, y1) and (x2, y2):y = y1 + [(x - x1) / (x2 - x1)] * (y2 - y1)

  • Correlation Coefficient (for Scatterplots) :
      Formula: r (Pearson's correlation coefficient) = (Σ[(x - x̄)(y - ȳ)]) / [√(Σ(x - x̄)^2) * √(Σ(y - ȳ)^2)]

Exponents and Radicals

  • Multiplication of Exponents :
       a^m * a^n = a^(m + n)

  • Division of Exponents: :
       a^m / a^n = a^(m - n)

  • power of a power :
       (a^m)^n = a^(m * n)

  • power of product :
       (a * b)^n = a^n * b^n

  • power of Quotient :
       (a / b)^n = a^n / b^n

  • Zero Exponent :
       a^0 = 1 (for a ≠ 0)

  • Negative Exponent :
       a^(-n) = 1 / a^n

  • Fractional Exponent: :
       a^(1/n) is the nth root of a.

Radicals

  • Product of Radicals :
      √a * √b = √(ab)

  • Quotient of Radicals :
      √a / √b = √(a/b)

  • Radical Exponent :
       √a^n = (√a)^n = a^(n/2)

  • Simplify Radical Expressions::
      √(a * a) = |a|, where |a| is the absolute value of a.

  • Conjugate in Rationalizing Denominators: :
       To rationalize a denominator with a square root, multiply both the numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator.

Logarithm

  • loga (xy) = loga x + loga y

  • loga (x/y) = loga x – loga y

  • logx x = 1

  • loga 1 = 0

  • loga (xn) = n(loga x)

  • loga x = 1/(logx a)

  • loga x = (logb x)/(logb a) = (log x)/(log a)
