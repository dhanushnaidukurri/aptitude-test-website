Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):

  • Purpose of EDA: EDA is the initial step in data analysis, aimed at summarizing the main characteristics of a dataset, identifying patterns, and detecting anomalies.

  • Data Visualization: EDA involves creating visualizations like histograms, box plots, scatter plots, and bar charts to gain insights into data distribution and relationships.

  • Descriptive Statistics: Calculate and interpret descriptive statistics such as mean, median, standard deviation, and percentiles to understand the central tendency and spread of the data.

  • Outlier Detection: Identify outliers or unusual data points that may require further investigation

  • Data Cleaning: EDA often reveals missing data and inconsistencies, leading to data cleaning processes to improve data quality

Hypothesis Testing:

  • Purpose of Hypothesis Testing: Hypothesis testing is used to determine if there is a significant difference or relationship between variables in a dataset.

  • Null and Alternative Hypotheses: Formulate null and alternative hypotheses to express the research question being tested.

  • Test Statistics: Calculate test statistics (e.g., t-test, chi-squared test) to evaluate whether the observed data provides enough evidence to reject the null hypothesis.

  • Significance Levels: Set a significance level (alpha) to determine the threshold for statistical significance.

  • Interpretation: Based on the test results, decide whether to accept or reject the null hypothesis and draw conclusions about the research question.

Regression Analysis:

  • Purpose of Regression Analysis: Regression analysis is used to understand and model the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables.

  • Linear Regression: Explore simple and multiple linear regression techniques to model linear relationships in data.

  • Non-linear Regression: Learn about non-linear regression models when the relationship between variables is not linear.

  • Coefficients and Predictions: Interpret regression coefficients and use them to make predictions or explain the impact of independent variables on the dependent variable.

  • Model Evaluation: Assess the quality of regression models through metrics like R-squared, mean squared error, and root mean squared error.

Clustering and Classification

  • Clustering: Understand clustering algorithms like K-means and hierarchical clustering used to group data points with similar characteristics.

  • Classification: Explore classification algorithms like decision trees, logistic regression, and support vector machines used to assign data points to predefined categories.

  • Feature Engineering: Select and preprocess features to improve the performance of clustering and classification models.

  • Model Evaluation: Assess the quality of clustering and classification models using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score.

  • Applications: Learn about real-world applications, such as customer segmentation (clustering) and spam email detection (classification).

Time Series Analysis:

  • Components of Time Series: Learn about the components of time series data, including trend, seasonality, and noise.

  • Time Series Data: Understand time series data, which involves observations collected at regular intervals over time.

  • Decomposition: Decompose time series data to isolate these components and analyze them separately.

  • Forecasting: Use time series analysis techniques to make predictions about future data points.

  • Moving Averages and Exponential Smoothing: Explore methods like moving averages and exponential smoothing for smoothing and forecasting time series data.
