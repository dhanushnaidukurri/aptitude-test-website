Formulas on Physics:

  • Newton's Laws: Newton's three laws of motion are fundamental principles in physics, explaining the relationship between force, mass, and motion

  • Electromagnetism: This field encompasses electricity and magnetism, including Maxwell's equations that describe the behavior of electric and magnetic fields.

  • Thermodynamics: Thermodynamics deals with heat, work, and energy transfer. The laws of thermodynamics govern processes in the physical world.

  • Quantum Mechanics: Quantum mechanics is a branch of physics that deals with the behavior of particles at the atomic and subatomic scales, involving concepts like wave-particle duality and quantization of energy levels

  • Relativity Theory: Albert Einstein's theory of relativity consists of special and general relativity, which describe the behavior of objects at high speeds and the effect of gravity on spacetime.

Formulas on Chemistry:

  • Chemical Reactions: Chemistry is the study of how atoms and molecules interact and transform through chemical reactions, involving concepts like stoichiometry and kinetics.

  • Periodic Table: The periodic table organizes elements by atomic number and chemical properties, providing valuable information about the elements.

  • Chemical Bonding: Chemical bonds, including covalent, ionic, and metallic bonds, are fundamental to understanding how atoms combine to form compounds.

  • Acids and Bases: Understanding the properties of acids and bases is crucial in chemistry, as they play a role in many chemical reactions and processes.

  • Chemical Equilibrium: Chemical equilibrium is a dynamic state where the rates of the forward and reverse reactions are equal, and it's essential for understanding reversible chemical processes.

Formulas on Biology:

  • Cell Theory: All living organisms are composed of cells, which are the basic units of life, and they follow the principles of cell theory.

  • Evolution: The theory of evolution by natural selection explains how species change over time in response to environmental pressures.

  • Genetics: Genetics explores the inheritance of traits from one generation to the next, including the structure and function of DNA.

  • Ecology: Ecology studies the relationships between organisms and their environments, addressing concepts like ecosystems, food webs, and biodiversity.

  • Classification and Taxonomy: Taxonomy is the science of classifying living organisms into a hierarchical system based on shared characteristics, with domains, kingdoms, phyla, classes, orders, families, genera, and species.

Earth Science

  • Plate Tectonics: The theory of plate tectonics explains the movement of Earth's lithospheric plates, leading to phenomena like earthquakes and volcanic activity.

  • Weather and Climate: Meteorology deals with short-term weather patterns, while climatology studies long-term climate trends and changes.

  • Geological Time Scale: Geology encompasses the study of Earth's history, including eons, eras, periods, and epochs.

  • Oceanography: Oceanography focuses on the study of Earth's oceans, including ocean currents, marine life, and the impact of human activities.

  • Environmental Science: Environmental science examines the interactions between humans and their environment, addressing issues like pollution, conservation, and sustainability.

Astronomy

  • Solar System: The solar system consists of the Sun, planets, moons, asteroids, and comets, with each having unique properties and characteristics.

  • Stellar Life Cycle: Stars go through a life cycle, from formation to death, with various stages like nebulae, main sequence, red giants, and supernovae.

  • Galaxies: Galaxies are vast collections of stars, gas, and dust, and the Milky Way is our home galaxy.

  • Cosmology: Cosmology is the study of the origin, evolution, and large-scale structure of the universe, including the Big Bang theory.

  • Exoplanets: Exoplanets are planets outside our solar system, and their discovery has opened up the search for potentially habitable worlds beyond Earth.
