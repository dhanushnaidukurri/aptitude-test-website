Exact Synonyms

  • Exact synonyms are words that share the same meaning and can be used interchangeably in all contexts

  • They are often used to avoid repetition in writing or to add variety to one's vocabulary

  • For example, "buy" and "purchase" are exact synonyms because they can be used in the same way

Partial Synonyms

  • Partial synonyms are words with similar meanings but are not entirely identical

  • They may have subtle differences in connotation or usage

  • For example, "happy" and "joyful" are partial synonyms, with "joyful" implying a more intense form of happiness

Formal And Informal Synonyms

  • Words can be categorized as formal or informal based on their suitability in different contexts

  • Formal synonyms are typically used in academic, professional, or official settings, while informal synonyms are used in casual or everyday language

  • For instance, "acquire" is formal, while "get" is informal

Complementary Antonyms

  • Complementary antonyms are pairs of antonyms where one term must be true if the other is false, with no middle ground

  • They represent mutually exclusive states or conditions

  • For example, a person is either "alive" or "dead"

Gradable Antonyms

  • Gradable antonyms are antonyms that exist on a scale or spectrum

  • They allow for degrees of difference between the two opposites

  • For instance, "hot" and "cold" can describe various temperatures in between
